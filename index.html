How it all Started?

I've always been known as that kid who draws and creates whenever they can. just the regular "art kid". there wasn't a single lesson in school, where I didn't sketch even a small silly doodles at the side of my notes; it helped me focus on something more enjoyable - because school was definitely not that, how surprising - and thus be able to hear the teacher in the background.

in elementary school, sketching slowly began to move to visual graphics, in the form of snapping photos of what caught my eye. always the nature, buildings, rarely capturing people, and these small, innocent hobbies turned into something that drags on with me for several years, till now. traditional drawing slowly lead to move to the digital form, from the beginning; as drawings in paint, gimp, later in sai, moving to drawing with a finger using a phone, to more professional conditions, i.e. a stylus + ipad and a paid program. the label stuck to me as a kid, which used to give me great motivation, slowly overwhelms me more and more with the time, that the whole environment, knowing that I draw, probably puts some faith on me to create something good. now, this patch just puts pressure on me because every year I make less and less; without abandoning this interest tho. currently I create mainly for myself. I take pictures on trips when I really think that something is worth capturing in a pleasant frame, and the drawing will only appear when I really feel like it, and not doing it just because of the force.

‚òÖ My Photography Likings

I absolutely love old architecture. beautiful buildings combined with the light glow of the golden hour sun, which incorporate browns and black, or crumbling ruins in a cloudier atmosphere later ripped from the colors at the editing stage, in monochromatic appearances. I guess you can clearly see it in my photos. I love capturing the threads of nature that have plants in them. also things that at first glance look ordinary, like warm coffee in a cup standing right next to a book, or an abandoned bicycle already overgrown with weeds on the sidelines of a field. next love is definitely street photography. people busy with themselves, walking through the city, all natural and unplanned.
